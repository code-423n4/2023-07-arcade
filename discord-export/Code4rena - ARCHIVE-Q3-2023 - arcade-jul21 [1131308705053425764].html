Priyam 07/24/2023 09:25
In the context of vesting contracts, a "cliff" refers to a specific period of time that must pass before any vested tokens or assets become accessible. During the cliff period, no tokens or assets are unlocked or available for the recipient. Typically, vesting contracts have a cliff period to ensure that the recipient remains committed to the project or organization for a certain duration before gaining access to their vested tokens. It acts as a form of lock-up period or probationary period. Once the cliff period is over, the vesting schedule begins, and a portion of the tokens or assets becomes accessible at regular intervals until the grant or vesting period ends. The cliff period is defined by specifying a block number or a specific duration of time in the vesting contract. It is important to note that during the cliff period, no tokens are unlocked, and the recipient does not have access to them. The purpose of the cliff period is to align the interests of the recipient with the project or organization and incentivize long-term commitment.
